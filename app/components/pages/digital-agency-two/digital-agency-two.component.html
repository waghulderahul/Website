Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Get answers to frequently asked questions about our services, installations, and more

  • What is a household EV charging station?

    A household EV charging station is a device installed at your home to charge your electric vehicle (EV). It allows you to recharge your EV's battery more quickly and conveniently than a standard wall outlet.

  • What are the types of home EV chargers?

    There are two main types of home EV chargers: Level 1 and Level 2. Level 1 chargers use a standard 120-volt outlet and are slower, taking around 8-12 hours to fully charge an EV. Level 2 chargers use a 240-volt outlet and can fully charge an EV in 4-6 hours.

  • How do I install a home EV charging station?

    Installation typically involves hiring a licensed electrician to ensure the charger is properly installed and meets local electrical codes. The electrician will set up the necessary electrical infrastructure and mount the charging unit.

  • Can I install an EV charging station myself?

    While some Level 1 chargers can be plugged into a standard outlet, it's generally recommended to have a professional install a Level 2 charger to ensure safety and compliance with local regulations.
